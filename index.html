Material Bank

Seconds: the stream
nobody talks about

30–40% of collected Finnish textiles are "Seconds" — too worn for direct reuse, too valuable to shred. HUPcycle routes this stream to designers and makers who can refurbish and remake it into high-value products.

Tier A
10–15%

Reuse

Good-condition clothing resold as-is via Lemppari store. High value per unit, limited volume.

Tier B — HUPcycle focus
30–40%
Seconds

Soiled, worn, imperfect — but valuable. Remake feedstock commands €2,000–10,000/tn. The untapped opportunity.

Tier C
30–35%

Recycling

End-of-life at €10–600/tn. Mechanical or chemical processing. HUPcycle intercepts upstream before this stage.

Maker onboarding — four steps
a.

Inquiry call

Specification discussion: what fibre types, volumes, and conditions suit the maker's practice.

b.

Test sorting

A test batch is prepared — confirming there is enough of the right feedstock to sustain production needs.

c.

Facility visit

Designer visits the LSJH facility: inspection and creative inspiration from the full material stream in person.

d.

Supply contract

Continuous supply agreement including permission for social media content using the Seconds origin story.

Industry context

The honest challenges — and why a hub changes the economics

Small sorting quantities

Individual makers can't justify sorting small batches alone. A centralised hub aggregates volume so the economics work at every scale.

Craft-heavy, time-intensive remake

→End products are expensive, one-of-a-kind, made-to-order. The right market positioning makes this a strength.

Niche products, niche buyers

→Story-driven product pages and before/after provenance connect unique pieces with conscious consumers seeking exactly this.

Technology & Infrastructure

Scale requires
automation.

The remake process is inherently craft-heavy. Moving from artisan-scale to industry-scale requires AI-assisted sorting, robotic manufacturing support, and digital traceability infrastructure aligned with EU EPR/ESPR requirements.

01
AI Sorting

Automated fibre identification & grading

Computer vision and spectroscopic analysis to automatically identify fibre composition and grade incoming Seconds material. Trosort's conveyor sorting system and Redivivum's Identify-Inspect-Index platform are active reference points. Estimated 60% reduction in manual sorting labour.

Referenced: Trosort · Redivivum · LSJH R&D programme
02
Robotics

Robotic support for remake manufacturing

Semi-automated cutting, deconstruction, and fabric preparation lets skilled makers focus on design decisions while repetitive preparation is handled at machine speed — enabling batch economics without sacrificing handcrafted quality.

Referenced: Losanje platform · LSJH webinar, 1 April 2026
03
Traceability

Digital Product Passport aligned with EU DPP

Every upcycled product needs a traceable link to its material origin — which LSJH hub, what fibre type, which maker. This satisfies incoming EU EPR/ESPR requirements and is the cornerstone of the "Upcycled in Finland" brand proposition commanding premium pricing.

Aligned with EU EPR 2026 · ESPR textile requirements
04
Content AI

AI-assisted product storytelling & marketing

Each upcycled piece is unique. Scaling the content-first marketplace requires automated generation of product stories, before/after documentation, and social content — connecting each piece to its material provenance in a format that resonates with conscious consumers.
